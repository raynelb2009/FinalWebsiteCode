A Bird In Flight - He Titi, e Rere

He Titi, e Rere - Maori Translation

Ka tū ake au...Ka tū ake au...i te tihi o moutere Ohinau e...i! He tītiī e rere, he tītī e ruku...i nga tai o Whitianga a Kupe!

Kia tere te haere ki te maunga tū tonu...te maunga Tāhanga, Pakawera, toketoke, taketake, ngā toki o ngā tūpuna. Te whatu o Poutini e...i. Arā... Riporipo te tai...tumutumu ngā wai, I runga onepū a Wharekaho... Ōhuka ki taitimu, taipare o Taputapuātea. Ka rērere atu rā ki Whangamāroro...e tū mai ana ko Te Ana... Ka tae maumahara k Wai-toto-rā...Aue! Ka heke atu au i te taumata ko Motukū te puke, Māringi noa puawai o te pōhutukawa...e...i...

Ka tāhuri nei au te wāhi Puahape...Tahura te tūpuna! He Wharetangata, he whare mātauranga...Tū tonu i te pō, tū tonu i te ao... I runga parirau a Hei... Hei Hā!

On the blessed wings of Hei I swift, indeed, I do! Composed by Michelle Lee and James Webster, 2008. This patere tells of places within the Te Whitianga-ā Hei area, shared by Joe Davis. Translation by Brandon Amoamo.

He Titi, e Rere - English Translation

Here I stand...Here I stand on the peak of Ohinau island! Like the mutton bird I fly...like the mutton bird I dive...into the swell of Whitianga's tides!

In bird form I travel, swiftly to the ancient mount of Tāhanga, To motherlode of finest basalt, source of exquisite anscestral adzes, tempered, crafted and expertly wrought, chipped and chinked from aboriginal stone. My bird recalls, the eye of monstrous Poutini, progenitor of Greenstone who fled, who fled... Over languorous swirls of ocean I soar...over corrugated rhythms of tide and sand, Upon the beach at Wharekaho... As tide ebbs at Ōhuka, the tide peaks at Taputapuātea. My flight carries me onto Whangamāroro, where there stands Te Ana... My thoughts return to those bloodier days when water ran red at Waitotorā...Alas! In flight I descend to the summit of Motukū, Where the delicate red bloom of pohutukawa spills like blood into the hallowed earth of my memories of this place...alas...

As I fly, I turn now to Puahape...the territory of that eponymous grand sire, Tahura! Further I glide, over the birthing grounds of the people, to the grounds of their minds... Ever present by night, ever present by day... On the blessed wings of Hei I swift, indeed, I do! Composed by Michelle Lee and James Webster, 2008. This patere tells of places within the Te Whitianga-ā-Hei area, shared by Joe Davis. Translation by Brandon Amoamo.
